Audition Q&A

1. What types of dances does LOKO do?

LOKO specializes in promoting Korean music and pop culture through K-Hiphop, K-Pop, and K-R&B dances! Because of this, we cover a variety of dance styles. However, we do dances to all songs including American pop, etc.

2. How often are LOKO's dance practices?

We typically have 2 hour practices twice a week. We also perform at a variety of events on campus, so we sometimes have an additional dress rehearsal for those events, but that varies week by week.

3. What is the audition process like in terms of learning days, the choreography length, and the optional piece?

There are 2 learning days offered usually at night and you must attend one in order to audition! You can expect to learn 30-40 seconds of choreography within 2 hours. Feel free to ask questions at learning day to clarify or go over the moves again. Coming to learning day does not mean you are required to audition. If you want to come to learning day just to learn new choreography, that's fine!

On audition day, we'll ask you to perform the audition piece in small groups on a first come first serve basis. After all the groups have gone, you can also showcase your personal style by performing an optional piece to a song of your choice; it does not have to be K-Pop or K-Hiphop related!

After the audition, if you passed, we will notify you and you will have a few days to decide to commit!

4. Can the optional piece be pre-learned or is it freestyle?

The optional piece is usually pre-learned and 1 minute maximum. It can be a cover, your own choreography, or a piece you've learned in the past. Essentially, it's a dance that you prepare on your own to show us you dance capabilities and strengths!

The optional piece can only boost you and you will not be penalized for not doing an optional piece.

5. Any tips for auditions?

Ask clarifying questions during the learning days, use the dance spaces on campus for extra practice before the audition (WSH 519/601/Garden Room, PSB), be confident and have fun during the audition, and remember that we don't expect the audition piece to be perfect!

6. How often do you hold auditions and how many people do you accept each semester?

We hold auditions within the first month of every semester, so twice a year in the Fall and Spring.

We do not place a cap on the amount of accepted auditionees!

7. Can you re-audition if you already did in the past?

Yes, of course you can and we encourage you to!

8. What are the responsibilities and time commitment of LOKO Productions?

LOKO Productions is an in-house production studio that produces 100% of LOKO's dance covers (including storyboarding, filming, and editing), photoshoots, apparel, and marketing content!

We commit ~4 hours a week on filming, editing, and other projects, but this highly varies based on the number of projects we do per semester.

Productions is meant to be a fun and homey team, so you won't have to expect putting in a massive number of hours!

9. What is the recruitment process like for the Productions Team?

There will be a short written application to be submitted via google forms and then an interview with the Productions Director.

10. Do you have to be Korean/Asian or have previous dance experience to be in LOKO?

No, definitely not!

LOKO is open to all students of all skill levels. We have many non-Korean and some non-Asian members that all share a love for dance and Korean culture. Many of our current members have not had any dance experience before coming to college!
